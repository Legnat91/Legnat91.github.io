Vocabulary

UNIDAD 1

Vocabulary for the workplace.

  1. Application: A request for a job.
  2. Colleague: A person who works with you.
  3. Deadline: The date by which something must be done.
  4. Interview: A meeting with a potential employer to discuss a job.
  5. Promotion: A raise in your job position and pay.
  6. Salary: The amount of money you are paid for your work.
  7. Sick leave: A period of time when you are not at work because you are ill.
  8. Vacation: A period of time when you are not at work for pleasure.
  9. Workplace: The place where you work.

UNIDAD 2

Project management.

  1. Deliverable: A specific outcome or result that must be completed within a certain period of time.
  2. Milestone: A key point in the project's timeline that marks the completion of a major task or phase.
  3. Stakeholder:A person or organization who has an interest in the project and its outcome.
  4. Scope: The range of work that must be completed for the project to be successful.
  5. Timeline: A plan that shows when each task in the project will be completed.
  6. Budget: An estimate of the amount of money that will be spent on the project.
  7. Risk: A potential event that could have a negative impact on the project.
  8. Issue: A problem or difficulty that arises during the project.
  9. To brainstorm: To generate many ideas quickly, often without judgmet.
  10. To prioritize: The rank tasks in order of importance, so that the most important task are completed first.
  11. To launch: The introduction of a new product or service to the market.

UNIDAD 3

All about customers.

  1. Empathy: The ability to understand and share the feelings and interests of another person or people.
  2. Active Listening: Giving full attention to someone, showing genuine interest and seeking to understan their thoughts and feelings.
  3. Business Transparency: Involver being open, honest and straight-forward about the company's operations and transactions.
  4. Social Reponsibility: Companies make an effort to operate in a way that positively and actively helps society and the world.
  5. Inclusion: Awareness of social and cultural diversity.
  6. Personalisation: Making an effort to cater to individual needs.
  7. Professional Ethics: Principles guiding conduct at work.
  8. Compromise: Principle by which something promised is delivered.

UNIDAD 4

Digital workspace vocabulary.

  1. Telecommuting: The practice of working from a location other than the office, often from home, using technology to stay connected.
  2. Virtual meetings: Online gatherings where participants can communicate and collaborate using video conferencing tools.
  3. Freelancer: An individual who works on a project basis for various clients, often as an independent contractor.
  4. Cybersecurity: Measures and practices to protect computer systems, networks, and data from digital attacks or unauthorised access.
  5. Remote collaboration: Working together on projects or tasks while physically distant, using online tools and platforms.
  6. Cloud computing: Storing and accessing data and programs over the Internet instead of a computer’s hard drive.
  7. Flexible hours: The ability to adjust working hours to suit one’s preferences or needs.
  8. Digital nomad: Someone who works remotely and travels, often relying on technology to stay connected.
  9. Online platform: A website or application that facilitates communication, collaboration, or the exchange of goods and services.
  10. Webinar: A seminar or workshop conducted over the Internet, allowing participants to join remotely.

UNIDAD 5

Business and sectors vocabulary.

  1. Automotive: The sector that deals with cars, trucks, and other vehicles.
  2. Technology: The industry related to computers, software, and electronic devices.
  3. Healthcare: The field focused on providing medical services and products.
  4. Hospitality: The sector that encompasses hotels, restaurants, and tourism.
  5. Finance: The industry involved in managing money, banking, and investments.
  6. Agriculture: The cultivation of the land and the production of food and crops.
  7. Manufacturing: The process of making goods or products on a large scale.
  8. Retail: The business of selling goods directly to consumers.
  9. Energy: The sector involving the production and distribution of power.
  10. Telecommunications: The field related to communication over long distances.

UNIDAD 6

Commerce concepts vocabulary.

  1. Arbitrage: The practice of buying and selling financial instruments in different markets to take advantage of price differences.
  2. Procurement: The process of obtaining goods and services for a business or organisation.
  3. Stockholder: A person who owns shares in a company, making them a partial owner
  4. Venture capital: The financial gain earned from an investment or business operation
  5. Collateral: Money or assets pledged to a lender as security for a loan.
  6. Globalisation: The process of integrating and interconnecting economies on a worldwide scale.
  7. Dividend: The total value of all goods and services produced by a country in a specific time period.
  8. Capitalism: The system in which businesses are owned and controlled by private individuals for profit.
  9. Insolvency: A situation where a company or individual cannot meet its financial obligations.
  10. Monopoly: The exclusive possession or control of the supply or trade in a commodity or service.

UNIDAD 7

Decoding the language of innovation.

  1. Artificial Intelligence (AI): Technology that simulates human intelligence processes through algorithms and computer systems.
  2. Augmented Reality (AR): Technology that overlays virtual elements onto the real world through devices like smartphones or special glasses.
  3. Blockchain: A decentralized ledger system that ensures the security and transparency of transactions.
  4. E-commerce: The buying and selling of goods and services through online platforms. E-commerce offers consumers the convenience of shopping from anywhere at any time, with access to a wide variety of products.
  5. User experience (UX): The perception and response of the user when interacting with a product or service.
  6. Personalization: The adaptation of products, services, and communications to individual consumer preferences.
  7. Omnichannel: A marketing strategy that integrates multiple communication and sales channels to provide a consistent and seamless customer experience. Consumers can interact with a brand through physical stores, online, mobile apps, and social media in an integrated manner.

UNIDAD 8

Security in Administration vocabulary.

  1. Access control: A system that restricts who can access certain information or resources.
  2. Authentication: The process of verifying a user's identity.
  3. Authorisation: The process of granting a user access to certain information or resources based on their identity and permissions.
  4. Data encryption: The process of converting data into a form that is unreadable without the correct decryption key.
  5. Firewall: A network security system that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic.
  6. Intrusion detection system: A system that monitors a network for signs of unauthorised activity.
  7. Intrusion prevention system: A system that actively blocks unauthorised activity on a network.
  8. Password policy: A set of rules that govern the creation and use of passwords.
  9. Social engineering: A technique that uses human psychology to trick people into revealing sensitive information or taking actions that they would not normally take.

UNIDAD 9

Digital communication vocabulary.

  1. Email: Electronic mail for sending and receiving messages.
  2. Video conferencing: Virtual meeting using video and audio technology.
  3. Instant messaging: Real-time messaging platform for quick communication.
  4. Project management software: Software for managing tasks and projects within a team.
  5. Cloud storage: Online storage space accessed over the internet.
  6. Social media: Online platforms for professional networking and information sharing.
  7. Webinar: Alive online seminar or presentation.
  8. Podcast: Audio files with episodic content, often educational or informative.
  9. Collaboration platform: A platform for online discussions and document sharing within a company.
  10. Intranet: Internal company network accessible only to authorised employees.
