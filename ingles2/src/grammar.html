Grammar

BLOQUE 1. Present Simple & Present Continuous

Present Simple

  • Forma:
    • He/She/It works
    • I/You/We/They work
    • Do/Does + sujeto para preguntas
    • Don’t/Doesn’t + verbo para negativas
  • Usos:
    • Hábitos y rutinas: I get up at 7:30.
    • Hechos permanentes: They live in London.
    • Verdades generales: Water boils at 100°C.
    • Horarios fijos: The train leaves at 9 p.m.

Present Continuous

  • Forma: am/is/are + verbo-ing
  • Usos:
    • Acciones ahora: She is talking.
    • Acciones temporales: I’m working in Madrid this month.
    • Planes ya organizados: We are meeting tomorrow.
    • Cambios: The weather is getting colder.

Adverbs of frequency

  • Always, usually, often, sometimes, hardly ever, never.
  • Orden:
    • Verbo normal: He often cooks.
    • Verbo be: She is always late.

Resumen

Uso Present Simple Present Continuous
Rutinas Sí No
Acciones ahora No Sí
Situaciones temporales No Sí
Horarios fijos Sí No

BLOQUE 2. Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Present Perfect

  • Forma: have/has + past participle
  • Usos:
    • Experiencias: Have you ever been to China?
    • Resultado ahora: I’ve broken my arm.
    • Tiempo no terminado: I’ve had three coffees today.
    • FOR/SINCE: He has lived here for 10 years / since 2015.

Past Simple

  • Forma: verbo en pasado (regular -ed o irregular)
  • Usos:
    • Acción pasada terminada: We met in 2010.
    • Decimos cuándo: I didn’t go to work yesterday.
    • Historias / secuencias: He came in, sat down and started working.

Diferencia clara

  • Present Perfect: no decimos cuándo, importa el resultado ahora.
  • Past Simple: decimos o preguntamos cuándo, tiempo pasado cerrado.

BLOQUE 3. Present for future, Articles, Stative verbs

Present for future

  • Present Simple: horarios fijos
    • The flight leaves at 11 a.m. tomorrow.
  • Present Continuous: planes decididos
    • We are meeting the client on Friday.

Articles

  • a/an: singular, primera mención, profesiones
    • She is a doctor.
  • the: algo concreto o único
    • the sun, the train, the hotel
  • Zero article: países, ciudades, comidas, conceptos generales
    • I like coffee.
    • She lives in Spain.

Stative verbs

  • Verbos de estado (no acción): know, like, love, believe, need, own, understand.
  • Normalmente no se usan en -ing:
    • Correcto: I know the answer.
    • Incorrecto: I am knowing the answer.

BLOQUE 4. Modal verbs

Obligation / Permission

  • Obligación:
    • must: obligación fuerte
    • have to: obligación externa / normas
  • No obligación:
    • don’t have to: no es necesario
  • Prohibición:
    • mustn’t, can’t: está prohibido
  • Permiso:
    • can (informal), may (formal), be allowed to

Probabilidad

  • must: casi seguro
    • He must be at work.
  • may / might / could: posible
    • She might be in a meeting.
  • can’t / couldn’t: imposible
    • He can’t be at home, the lights are off.

Ability y modal perfect

  • can: habilidad presente (I can swim.)
  • could: habilidad pasada (I could swim when I was 7.)
  • be able to: cualquier tiempo.
  • must have / may have / could have / should have + participio: probabilidad o crítica en el pasado.

BLOQUE 5. Past Simple, Past Continuous, Past Perfect

Past Simple

  • Forma: verbo en pasado (worked, went).
  • Uso: acciones completas en el pasado.
    • We met in 2007.

Past Continuous

  • Forma: was/were + verbo-ing.
  • Uso:
    • Acción en progreso en un momento del pasado: At 7 I was having breakfast.
    • Acción larga interrumpida: I was having a shower when the phone rang.

Past Perfect

  • Forma: had + past participle.
  • Uso: acción anterior a otra acción pasada:
    • When I arrived, she had already left.

BLOQUE 6. Direct & Indirect Questions, Superlatives

Direct questions

  • Estructura: Wh-word + aux + sujeto + verbo
    • Where do you live?
    • Do you like this shirt?

Indirect questions

  • Más formales: llevan frase introductoria.
  • Ejemplos:
    • Can you tell me where you live?
    • I’d like to know what time the shop opens.

Superlatives

  • Uso: comparar con todo el grupo:
    • He is the tallest in the class.
  • Formación básica:
    • short adj: tall → the tallest
    • -y: happy → the happiest
    • largo: the most interesting
    • irregulares: good → the best

BLOQUE 7. Adjective order y Conditionals

Adjective order (OSASCOMP)

  • Orden: Opinion – Size – Age – Shape – Color – Origin – Material – Purpose.
  • Ejemplo:
    • a beautiful big old round wooden table

Conditionals

  • First Conditional:
    • If + present → will + infinitive
    • If it rains, we will stay at home.
  • Second Conditional:
    • If + past simple → would + infinitive
    • If I had more time, I would travel more.

BLOQUE 8. Relative Clauses & Direct / Indirect Questions

Defining relative clauses

  • No llevan comas.
  • La información es NECESARIA para saber de qué persona/cosa hablamos.
    • I called the brother who lives in Ontario.
    • That’s the mug which/that she likes best.
  • THAT puede sustituir a who / which:
    • I didn’t know the man who/that was there.
  • Podemos omitir who / which / that cuando son objeto y van seguidos de sujeto + verbo:
    • Do you like the song (which/that) I wrote?
    • That’s the man (who/that) I like.

Non-defining relative clauses

  • Van SIEMPRE entre comas.
  • Añaden información EXTRA, no necesaria para identificar a la persona/cosa.
    • My brother, who lives in Ontario, is older.
  • No se puede usar that (solo who / which / whose / where / when).
  • No se puede omitir el pronombre relativo:
    • They introduced me to John, who I liked immediately. (✗ Not: to John, I liked…)

Defining vs Non-defining – Resumen

Característica Defining Non-defining
Comas No Sí, antes y después de la cláusula
Tipo de información Esencial para identificar al nombre Extra, se puede eliminar
Uso de THAT Sí (en lugar de who/which) No (solo who/which/whose…)
Omitir pronombre Sí, si es objeto (…(that) I saw) No, nunca

Direct vs Indirect Questions

  • Direct questions: orden de pregunta normal.
    • Where is Tondo Street?
    • What time does the supermarket open?
  • Indirect questions: más formales y educadas.
    • Frase introductoria + palabra interrogativa (o if/whether) + orden afirmativo (sujeto + verbo) + punto.
    • Can you tell me where Tondo Street is?
    • Do you know what time the supermarket opens?

WH-questions

Direct Indirect
Where is Tondo Street? Can you tell me where Tondo Street is?
What time does the supermarket open? Do you know what time the supermarket opens?
How much do you earn? Could you tell me how much you earn?
What is he doing? May I ask what he is doing?
What is your last name? May I know what your last name is?

Yes / No questions

Direct Indirect (if / whether + afirmativa)
Are you living in London? I’d like to know if you are living in London.
Does John like flying? Do you know whether John likes flying?
Did she go shopping? I’m not sure if she went shopping.
Was she a teacher? Can you tell me whether she was a teacher?

Frases introductorias típicas

  • Could you tell me…?
  • Do you know…?
  • I’d like to know…
  • I’d be interested to know…
  • May I ask…?

BLOQUE 9. Active & Passive Voice

Active voice

  • Estructura básica: Subject + Verb + Object.
    • The company delivers the products.
    • They have finished the report.
  • El sujeto realiza la acción.

Passive voice

  • Estructura básica: Object + be + past participle (+ by + agent).
    • The products are delivered (by the company).
    • The report has been finished (by them).
  • Se usa cuando:
    • La acción es más importante que quién la hace:
      • The invoice was sent yesterday.
    • No sabemos / no importa quién realiza la acción:
      • The documents were stolen.
  • El agente (by + persona / cosa) se puede omitir:
    • The meeting was cancelled. (by the manager)

Active vs Passive – Formas principales

Tiempo Active Passive
Present Simple They send the emails. The emails are sent.
Past Simple They signed the contract. The contract was signed.
Present Continuous They are preparing the report. The report is being prepared.
Past Continuous They were checking the accounts. The accounts were being checked.
Present Perfect They have updated the website. The website has been updated.
Future (will) They will send the documents. The documents will be sent.

Cómo pasar de active a passive

  • 1. El objeto de la oración activa pasa a ser sujeto en pasiva.
    • Active: The manager signed the contract.
    • Passive: The contract was signed (by the manager).
  • 2. El verbo principal pasa a participio pasado (signed, done, sent…).
  • 3. Se añade el verbo be en el tiempo adecuado (is, was, has been, will be…).
  • 4. El sujeto de la oración activa se convierte en agente con by o se omite.

Expresiones típicas en pasiva (contexto profesional)

  • The invoice will be sent tomorrow.
  • The equipment is checked every month.
  • All complaints are recorded in the system.
  • The decision has been made by the board.
