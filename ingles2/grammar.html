Grammar

BLOQUE 1. Present Simple & Present Continuous

Present Simple

  • Forma:
    • He/She/It works
    • I/You/We/They work
    • Do/Does + sujeto para preguntas
    • Don’t/Doesn’t + verbo para negativas
  • Usos:
    • Hábitos y rutinas: I get up at 7:30.
    • Hechos permanentes: They live in London.
    • Verdades generales: Water boils at 100°C.
    • Horarios fijos: The train leaves at 9 p.m.

Present Continuous

  • Forma: am/is/are + verbo-ing
  • Usos:
    • Acciones ahora: She is talking.
    • Acciones temporales: I’m working in Madrid this month.
    • Planes ya organizados: We are meeting tomorrow.
    • Cambios: The weather is getting colder.

Adverbs of frequency

  • Always, usually, often, sometimes, hardly ever, never.
  • Orden:
    • Verbo normal: He often cooks.
    • Verbo be: She is always late.

BLOQUE 2. Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Present Perfect

  • Forma: have/has + past participle
  • Usos:
    • Experiencias: Have you ever been to China?
    • Resultado ahora: I’ve broken my arm.
    • Tiempo no terminado: I’ve had three coffees today.
    • FOR/SINCE: He has lived here for 10 years / since 2015.

Past Simple

  • Forma: verbo en pasado (regular -ed o irregular)
  • Usos:
    • Acción pasada terminada: We met in 2010.
    • Decimos cuándo: I didn’t go to work yesterday.
    • Historias / secuencias: He came in, sat down and started working.

Diferencia clara

  • Present Perfect: no decimos cuándo, importa el resultado ahora.
  • Past Simple: decimos o preguntamos cuándo, tiempo pasado cerrado.

BLOQUE 3. Present for future, Articles, Stative verbs

Present for future

  • Present Simple: horarios fijos
    • The flight leaves at 11 a.m. tomorrow.
  • Present Continuous: planes decididos
    • We are meeting the client on Friday.

Articles

  • a/an: singular, primera mención, profesiones
    • She is a doctor.
  • the: algo concreto o único
    • the sun, the train, the hotel
  • Zero article: países, ciudades, comidas, conceptos generales
    • I like coffee.
    • She lives in Spain.

Stative verbs

  • Verbos de estado (no acción): know, like, love, believe, need, own, understand.
  • Normalmente no se usan en -ing:
    • Correcto: I know the answer.
    • Incorrecto: I am knowing the answer.

BLOQUE 4. Modal verbs

Obligation / Permission

  • Obligación:
    • must: obligación fuerte
    • have to: obligación externa / normas
  • No obligación:
    • don’t have to: no es necesario
  • Prohibición:
    • mustn’t, can’t: está prohibido
  • Permiso:
    • can (informal), may (formal), be allowed to

Probabilidad

  • must: casi seguro
    • He must be at work.
  • may / might / could: posible
    • She might be in a meeting.
  • can’t / couldn’t: imposible
    • He can’t be at home, the lights are off.

Ability y modal perfect

  • can: habilidad presente (I can swim.)
  • could: habilidad pasada (I could swim when I was 7.)
  • be able to: cualquier tiempo.
  • must have / may have / could have / should have + participio: probabilidad o crítica en el pasado.

BLOQUE 5. Past Simple, Past Continuous, Past Perfect

Past Simple

  • Forma: verbo en pasado (worked, went).
  • Uso: acciones completas en el pasado.
    • We met in 2007.

Past Continuous

  • Forma: was/were + verbo-ing.
  • Uso:
    • Acción en progreso en un momento del pasado: At 7 I was having breakfast.
    • Acción larga interrumpida: I was having a shower when the phone rang.

Past Perfect

  • Forma: had + past participle.
  • Uso: acción anterior a otra acción pasada:
    • When I arrived, she had already left.

BLOQUE 6. Direct & Indirect Questions, Superlatives

Direct questions

  • Estructura: Wh-word + aux + sujeto + verbo
    • Where do you live?
    • Do you like this shirt?

Indirect questions

  • Más formales: llevan frase introductoria.
  • Ejemplos:
    • Can you tell me where you live?
    • I’d like to know what time the shop opens.

Superlatives

  • Uso: comparar con todo el grupo:
    • He is the tallest in the class.
  • Formación básica:
    • short adj: tall → the tallest
    • -y: happy → the happiest
    • largo: the most interesting
    • irregulares: good → the best

BLOQUE 7. Adjective order y Conditionals

Adjective order (OSASCOMP)

  • Orden: Opinion – Size – Age – Shape – Color – Origin – Material – Purpose.
  • Ejemplo:
    • a beautiful big old round wooden table

Conditionals

  • First Conditional:
    • If + present → will + infinitive
    • If it rains, we will stay at home.
  • Second Conditional:
    • If + past simple → would + infinitive
    • If I had more time, I would travel more.
