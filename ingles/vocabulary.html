VOCABULARY

Principal

Workplace environment

  1. Technology: The use of science and machines to make tasks easier or more efficient.
  2. Accessible: Easy to reach or obtain, available for use.
  3. Lifelong learning: The process of gaining knowledge and skills throughout your entire life, not just during your formal education.
  4. Skills: Abilities and knowledge that you have developed through practice and experience.
  5. Tools: Instruments or devices used to perform specific tasks or achieve goals.
  6. Workforce: The group of people who are employed by a company or engaged in work.
  7. To be aware of: Having the knowledge or understanding of asituation, issue, or fact.
  8. Sustainability: Using resources and making choices in a way that doesn’t harm the environment and can be maintained for the future.
  9. Environmental protection: Actions taken to prevent harm to the environment and preserve natural resources.
  10. Diversity and inclusivity: Embracing and valuing differences among people, such as differences in race, culture, gender, and background, and ensuring that everyone is treated fairly and has equal opportunities.

Business essentials

  1. Mission: Is the main purpose of a company, explaining why it exists and what it aims to do.
  2. Vision: is a company's big dream for the future, showing what it wants to achieve in the long run.
  3. Smart marketing: means using clever and effective ways to tell people about a company's products or services to increase sales.
  4. Determination: Is the strong will and commitment to keep tryping and not give up, even when things are hard.
  5. Staying focused: Means keeping your attention on what you need to do without getting distracted by other things.
  6. Adapting to changes: Means being able to adjust your plans and action when new situations or problems happen.
  7. Passion: Is a very strong feeling of enthusiasm or excitement for something you do.
  8. Networking: Is the process or meeting and talking to other peopke to share information and help each other in business.
  9. Continuous improvement:Is always trying to make things better, never stopping to look for ways to improve work or products
  10. Customer feedback: Is the opinions and comments from people who buy your products or use your services, which can help a company improve.

Working in Procurement

  1. Chief procurement officer (CPO): Head the procurement team, decide on purchases, and negotiate with major suppliers.
  2. Procurement managers: Look after varied purchases like computers or office supplies, plan area-specific buying, and coordinate with other departaments for their needs.
  3. Category specialists/Buyers: Select top suppliers, negotiate prices and orders, and procure necessary items for the company.
  4. Procurement Analysts: Analyze procurement data and market trends, assist with reports for informed purchasing, and monitor buying effectiveness.
